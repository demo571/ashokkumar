Avdhut Gita
Avdhut Gita

About the Book

The Avadhut Gita is one of the most profound and radical spiritual texts in the Advaita Vedanta tradition. Composed by the sage Dattatreya, it delivers powerful insights into the nature of the Self and proclaims the ultimate truth of non-duality—that the individual soul and the universal Self are one.

This scripture is unique in that it doesn't follow a narrative or dialogue format. Instead, it is a spontaneous outpouring of spiritual realization, where the sage speaks directly from the state of liberation (moksha), beyond all concepts, rituals, and identities.

Yam Gita
Yam Gita

About the Book

The Yam Gita is a sacred dialogue attributed to Lord Yama, the deity of death and righteousness in Hindu tradition. It imparts profound teachings on the nature of the soul, life after death, and the path to liberation through knowledge and virtue.

This text emphasizes the impermanence of the physical body and the eternal nature of the atman (soul). Lord Yama explains that true wisdom lies in understanding this eternal truth and detaching from the illusions of the material world.

About the Book

"Kamal Ka Jadu" is a captivating concept rooted in ancient spiritual symbolism, often associated with the transformative and divine nature of the lotus (Kamal). In many Indian traditions, the lotus represents purity, awakening, and the magical power of inner growth amidst worldly challenges.

The idea explores how, like the lotus blooming beautifully in muddy water, human consciousness can rise above suffering, distractions, and limitations. This “jadu” or magic is the inner power to stay untouched by negativity and still radiate light, peace, and strength.
